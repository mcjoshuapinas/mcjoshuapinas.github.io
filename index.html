PhD. Joshua Piñas

I hold a Ph.D. in Engineering Science from Université Paris-Est Créteil (UPEC). During my doctoral studies, I developed an innovative method to identify meteorological sequences that lead to inadequate indoor temperatures in homes.

I have strong expertise in building physics modeling and simulation, which I use to conduct thermal, energetic, and environmental analyses of buildings.

Additionally, I possess extensive knowledge of the thermal, mechanical, and physical properties of solid construction materials.

I also hold a Master’s Degree in Physics. My research was conducted in collaboration between CERTES (UPEC), France, and the National University of Engineering (UNI), Peru.

For my master's thesis, I used COMSOL Multiphysics to evaluate, model, and simulate the LAMBDA-UNI, Portable LAMBDA-UNI, and Portable LAMBDA-CERTES devices. Additionally, I was involved in the patent process for the Portable LAMBDA-UNI.

Finally, I earned my degree in Physical Engineering by studying the thermal, mechanical, morphological, and structural properties of adobe as a biocomposite material. Furthermore, I initiated the design and construction of the LAMBDA-UNI according to ASTM C177 and C1043 standards.
